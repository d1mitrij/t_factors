## 1. Enterprise-Wide Scenario Framework

### Scenarios Used Across Playbooks

| Playbook | Scenario Use | Key Outputs |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **PB3 (Vulnerability)** | Define VF adjustment by scenario | Asset vulnerability (SSP1 vs SSP5) |
| **PB4 (Governance)** | Board approval, risk appetite linkage | Scenario governance & authority |
| **PB5 (Attribution)** | Portfolio-level scenario aggregation | Portfolio impact by scenario |
| **PB6 (Finance)** | TIR calculation, stress testing | Contingent liabilities by scenario |
| **PB7 (This)** | Strategic decision-making, target setting | Transition pathways, capital allocation |

### Recommended Standard Scenario Set

**Minimum 3 Scenarios** (TCFD/ISSB Requirement):

1. **Orderly Transition** (SSP1-1.9 / NGFS Net Zero 2050)
   - Immediate, ambitious climate action
   - Strong nature governance (TNFD Nature Positive)
   - **Use for**: Target setting, best-case planning

2. **Disorderly Transition** (SSP2-4.5 / NGFS Delayed Transition)
   - Delayed action, sudden policy shifts (2030+)
   - Moderate nature protection
   - **Use for**: Stress testing, risk management

3. **Hot House World** (SSP5-8.5 / NGFS Current Policies)
   - Limited climate action, high physical risk
   - Nature degradation accelerates
   - **Use for**: Worst-case stress, physical risk assessment

**Optional 4th Scenario** (Recommended for Complex Portfolios):
4. **Just Transition / Fragmentated** (SSP3-7.0)
   - Regional divergence, uneven policy
   - Social inequality increases
   - **Use for**: Social impact assessment, geopolitical risk

---

### Scenario Selection Process

**Step 1: Materiality Screening** (2-4 weeks)
- Which impacts are material to portfolio? (GHG, water, land, OHS)
- Which scenarios materially differentiate these impacts?
- **Output**: Priority scenarios (typically 3-4 from standard set)

**Step 2: Stakeholder Input** (1-2 weeks)
- Board: Risk appetite alignment
- Investors: Fiduciary duty, ESG expectations
- Regulators: Compliance (TCFD, TNFD, ESRS mandates)
- **Output**: Stakeholder-validated scenario selection

**Step 3: Governance Approval** (1 week)
- **Scenario Committee** review (CRO, CSO, CIO, CFO)
- **Board approval** (formally documented)
- **Communication**: Internal (staff training) + External (disclosure)

**Step 4: Implementation** (Ongoing)
- Integrate scenarios into all playbooks (PB3-6)
- Annual refresh of scenario assumptions (carbon prices, AWARE factors, etc.)
- 3-5 year major review (align to IPCC/NGFS updates)

## PART B: MATERIALITY ASSESSMENT (Cross-Cutting from PB1, PB2, PB6)

---

## 1. Double Materiality Framework (ESRS)

### Definition

**Impact Materiality** (Outside-In):
- Significant actual or potential impacts of the entity on the environment or society
- Assessed via Playbook 1 (IVF) and Playbook 2 (EF)

**Financial Materiality** (Inside-Out):
- Risks or opportunities that affect the entity's financial performance
- Assessed via Playbook 6 (TIR, dependency analysis)

**Double Materiality**:
- When an impact/dependency is BOTH impact-material AND financially material

---

### Materiality Assessment Process

**Step 1: Impact Materiality Assessment** (Playbooks 1-2)

**Quantitative Thresholds**:
| Threshold Level | Annual Impact (Portfolio) | Interpretation |
|----------------|--------------------------|----------------|
| **Very High** | >$500M or >10% of revenue | Automatically material |
| **High** | $100M-$500M or 2-10% revenue | Material unless exceptional |
| **Medium** | $10M-$100M or 0.2-2% revenue | Material if stakeholder concern or trend |
| **Low** | <$10M or <0.2% revenue | Not material unless required by law |

**Qualitative Factors**:
- Severity (irreversible damage, human rights violations)
- Scope (number of people/ecosystems affected)
- Stakeholder salience (investor, regulator, civil society concern)

**Example (Mining Company)**:
- **Land Use Impact**: 10,000 ha conversion/year × $1M/ha = **$10 billion/year**
  - **Materiality**: VERY HIGH (>$500M, >10% revenue, biodiversity hotspots)
  - **Conclusion**: Impact-material → ESRS E4 disclosure required

---

**Step 2: Financial Materiality Assessment** (Playbook 6 - TIR)

**Quantitative Thresholds**:
| TIR Range | Financial Materiality | Action Required |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------|
| **>0.8** | Very High (approaching full internalization) | Mandatory disclosure + financial effects |
| **0.5-0.8** | High | Disclosure + scenario analysis |
| **0.2-0.5** | Medium | Disclosure, monitor trends |
| **<0.2** | Low (mostly externalized) | Discretionary disclosure |

**Example Continued (Mining Company)**:
- **Land Use Dependency** (SSP1-1.9, 2030):
  - EUDR enforcement: 100% of production to EU market at risk if deforestation after 2020
  - Revenue at risk: $5 billion/year (50% of total revenue)
  - **TIR**: $5B / $10B = 0.5 (50% internalization via market exclusion)
  - **Materiality**: HIGH financial materiality

**Double Materiality Outcome**:
- ✓ Impact-material (VERY HIGH)
- ✓ Financial-material (HIGH)
- → **MANDATORY full ESRS disclosure + financial quantification + transition plan**

---

**Step 3: Materiality Matrix**

```
          Financial Materiality →
                   Low    Medium   High    Very High
Impact     ┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
Material   │                                      │
↓          │                                      │
Very High  │   DISC   DISC+FA  DISC+FA+TP  MAND  │
High       │   DISC    DISC     DISC+FA   DISC+FA│
Medium     │   MON     DISC      DISC      DISC  │
Low        │   --      MON       DISC      DISC  │
           └──────────────────────────────────────┘

Legend:
MAND = Mandatory full disclosure
DISC+FA+TP = Disclosure + Financial Analysis + Transition Plan
DISC+FA = Disclosure + Financial Analysis

MON = Monitor, discretionary disclosure
-- = Not material, no disclosure required
```

## PART C: TARGET SETTING & COMMITMENTS

---

## 1. Climate Targets (SBTi Framework)

### Near-Term Targets (2030)

**Scope 1+2 Target**:
```
Reduction_Target = Baseline_Emissions × (1 - SBTi_Reduction_Rate)

Where:
SBTi_Reduction_Rate (1.5°C pathway):
- 2030: 50% reduction vs. 2019 baseline (linear trajectory)
- 2040: 75% reduction
- 2050: 90% reduction (residual 10% via permanent carbon removal)
```

**Example (Manufacturing Company)**:
- Baseline (2019): 1,000,000 tonnes CO₂e (Scope 1+2)
- **2030 Target**: 500,000 tonnes (50% reduction)
- **2050 Target**: 100,000 tonnes (90% reduction) + 100,000 tonnes CDR (net-zero)

**Scope 3 Target** (Value Chain):
```
Scope3_Target = Baseline × (1 - Sector_Pathway_Rate)

Where:
Sector_Pathway varies (e.g., 30% for oil & gas, 50% for cement)
```

---

### Net-Zero Commitment

**Definition** (SBTi Net-Zero Standard):
- **Abatement**: Reduce emissions by 90% (Scope 1+2+3) vs. baseline
- **Neutralization**: Permanent carbon dioxide removal (CDR) for residual 10%
  - Acceptable: DACCS, BECCS, enhanced weathering, afforestation (if permanent)
  - NOT Acceptable: Avoided emissions offsets, renewable energy credits (unbundled)

**Target Year**: Typically 2050 (align to Paris Agreement)

---

## 2. Nature Targets (SBTN Framework)

### Land Use Target

**No Net Loss / Net Gain**:
```
Net_Land_Impact = Land_Converted - Land_Restored

Target: Net_Land_Impact ≤ 0 by 2030 (no net loss)
        Net_Land_Impact < 0 by 2040 (net positive)
```

**Example (Agriculture Company)**:
- **Baseline** (2025): 5,000 ha/year conversion (supply chain expansion)
- **Target** (2030): 0 ha/year conversion (100% intensification strategy)
- **Target** (2040): -2,000 ha/year (net restoration via degraded land recovery)

---

### Freshwater Target

**Water Stewardship in High-Stress Basins**:
```
Water_Target = Σ (Water_Consumption[Basin] × AWARE[Basin])

Target: Reduce AWARE-weighted water use by 50% in high-stress basins (AWARE >20) by 2030
```

**Example (Beverage Company)**:
- **Baseline**: 10M m³/year in high-stress basins (India, Spain, California)
- **Target**: 5M m³/year (via recycling, efficiency, sourcing shift)

---

### Biodiversity Target

**Emerging Framework** (SBTN Step 4 - pending):
- **No conversion** of high-biodiversity-value areas (HCVAs) from 2025
- **Species Habitat Index**: Maintain or improve SHI in material locations
- **Biodiversity offsets**: If conversion unavoidable, 10:1 ratio in equivalent habitat

---

## 3. Social Targets

### OHS Target

**Zero Harm Commitment**:
```
Target: Achieve zero fatalities by [Year]
        Reduce LTIR (Lost Time Injury Rate) to <1.0 per million hours by 2030
```

**Example (Construction Company)**:
- **Baseline**: LTIR = 3.5 (2025)
- **Pathway**:
  - 2027: LTIR = 2.0 (automation, training)
  - 2030: LTIR = 1.0 (target achieved)

---

### Living Wage Target

**Fair Compensation**:
```
Target: 100% of employees and Tier 1 suppliers paid living wage by 2030
```

**Measurement**: Living Wage benchmarks (Global Living Wage Coalition, Anker Methodology)

## PART D: TRANSITION PLANNING & PATHWAYS

---

## 1. Transition Plan Framework (TCFD / ISSB)

### Components

1. **Baseline** (2025): Current emissions, impact, TIR
2. **Targets** (2030, 2040, 2050): From Part C above
3. **Pathway**: Annual milestones, technology deployment, policy assumptions
4. **Capex Plan**: Investment requirements by year
5. **Financial Implications**: Earnings impact, capex, OpEx evolution
6. **Governance**: Accountability, review cycles, compensation linkage

---

### Example: Steel Company Transition Plan (BF-BOF → H2-DRI)

**Baseline (2025)**:
- Technology: 100% BF-BOF (Blast Furnace - Basic Oxygen Furnace)
- Emissions: 2.4 tonnes CO₂/tonne steel
- Production: 5M tonnes/year → 12M tonnes CO₂/year
- TIR (SSP1-1.9, 2030): 1.5 (carbon cost exceeds profitability)

**Target (2050)**:
- Technology: 90% H2-DRI (Hydrogen Direct Reduced Iron), 10% recycled (EAF)
- Emissions: 0.2 tonnes CO₂/tonne steel (90% reduction)
- Production: 5M tonnes/year → 1M tonnes CO₂/year
- TIR: -2.0 (opportunity, green steel premium)

**Pathway (Milestones)**:

| Year | Technology Mix | Capex (Cumulative) | Emissions | TIR | Key Assumption |
|------|---------------|-------------------|-----------|-----|----------------|
| 2025 | 100% BF-BOF | - | 12M tonnes | 1.5 | Baseline |
| 2030 | 80% BF-BOF, 20% H2-DRI | €500M | 10M tonnes | 1.2 | Green H2 subsidy active |
| 2035 | 50% BF-BOF, 50% H2-DRI | €2B | 6.5M tonnes | 0.8 | H2 cost parity |
| 2040 | 20% BF-BOF, 80% H2-DRI | €4B | 3M tonnes | -0.5 | Green steel premium |
| 2050 | 10% EAF, 90% H2-DRI | €5B | 1M tonnes | -2.0 | BF-BOF fully retired |

**Financial Implications**:

**Capex**:
- Total: €5 billion over 25 years
- Annual average: €200M/year

**OpEx Evolution**:
- **Baseline (BF-BOF, 2025)**: €50/tonne (energy, raw materials)
- **Transition (H2-DRI, 2030)**: €70/tonne (higher H2 cost, offset by carbon savings)
- **Target (H2-DRI, 2050)**: €55/tonne (H2 cost parity, green premium revenue)

**Carbon Cost Savings** (SSP1-1.9):
- **2025**: 12M tonnes × €100/tonne = €1.2B/year (carbon cost paid)
- **2050**: 1M tonnes × €300/tonne = €300M/year (89% reduction in carbon cost)
- **Cumulative savings (2025-2050)**: ~€20B (NPV at 5% discount)

**Net Financial Impact**:
- Capex: -€5B
- Carbon savings: +€20B (NPV)
- Green premium: +€2B (NPV, assuming 5% price premium on green steel)
- **Net**: +€17B (positive business case)

**Governance**:
- **Accountability**: CEO + CFO (joint target ownership)
- **Review**: Quarterly progress to Board Sustainability Committee
- **Compensation**: 20% of executive bonus linked to decarbonization milestones

## 2. Portfolio-Level Transition Pathway

### Aggregation Across Holdings

**Example: Investment Portfolio (Multi-Asset, Multi-Sector)**

**Target**: Portfolio carbon intensity reduction of 50% by 2030 vs. 2020 baseline

**Baseline (2020)**:
- **Portfolio Carbon Footprint** (financed emissions): 10M tonnes CO₂e
- **Portfolio Value**: $10B
- **Carbon Intensity**: 1,000 tonnes CO₂e / $M invested

**Pathway Components**:

1. **Engagement** (40% of target):
   - Engage top 20 holdings (80% of footprint) on SBTi targets
   - Assume 30% emissions reduction by 2030 via engagement
   - **Contribution**: 0.3 × 8M tonnes = 2.4M tonnes reduction

2. **Divestment** (30% of target):
   - Divest high-carbon, no-transition-plan assets (coal, oil sands)
   - 10% of portfolio value, 20% of emissions (2M tonnes)
   - **Contribution**: 2M tonnes reduction (but reinvest proceeds)

3. **Green Investment** (30% of target):
   - Reallocate divested capital to renewables, green tech
   - 10% of portfolio ($1B) → Low-carbon assets (avg 50 tonnes/$M)
   - **Contribution**: Avoid 1M tonnes vs. high-carbon reinvestment (delta = 1M tonnes)

**Total Reduction**:
- Engagement: -2.4M tonnes
- Divestment: -2M tonnes
- Green investment: -1M tonnes (avoided vs. counterfactual)
- **Total**: -5.4M tonnes (54% reduction, exceeds 50% target)

**2030 Portfolio**:
- Emissions: 10M - 5.4M = 4.6M tonnes
- Value: $12B (assuming 4% growth)
- **Carbon Intensity**: 4.6M / $12B = 383 tonnes/$M (62% reduction vs. 1,000)

## PART E: CAPITAL ALLOCATION & INVESTMENT STRATEGY

---

## 1. Impact-Adjusted Return on Capital

### Formula

```
Impact-Adjusted_ROIC = (NOPAT - Impact_Cost) / Invested_Capital

Where:
- NOPAT: Net Operating Profit After Tax
- Impact_Cost: Portfolio Impact (from PB5) × Internalization Rate (from PB6 TIR)
```

### Example: Two Competing Investments

**Investment A: Coal Power Plant (New Build)**:
- NOPAT: $100M/year
- Invested Capital: $500M
- **Traditional ROIC**: $100M / $500M = 20%
- **Impact** (from PB1-2): $500M/year (GHG @ SCC)
- **TIR** (SSP1-1.9): 1.5 (150% internalization → carbon cost exceeds SCC)
- **Impact Cost**: $500M × 1.5 = $750M/year (fully internalized)
- **Impact-Adjusted ROIC**: ($100M - $750M) / $500M = **-130%** (value-destroying)

**Investment B: Solar Farm**:
- NOPAT: $50M/year
- Invested Capital: $500M
- **Traditional ROIC**: $50M / $500M = 10%
- **Impact** (negative, displaces coal): -$100M/year (climate benefit)
- **TIR**: -2.0 (opportunity, green revenue premium)
- **Impact Cost**: -$100M × -2.0 = +$200M/year (value-creating)
- **Impact-Adjusted ROIC**: ($50M + $200M) / $500M = **50%** (high value-creating)

**Decision**: Invest in Solar (B), reject Coal (A)

## 2. Portfolio Optimization with Impact Constraints

### Framework

**Objective Function**:
```
Maximize: Risk-Adjusted Return
Subject to:
  1. Portfolio Impact ≤ Impact_Budget (from risk appetite, PB4)
  2. Portfolio TIR ≤ TIR_Max (e.g., 0.4, from risk appetite)
  3. Sectoral limits (e.g., <10% fossil fuels)
  4. Liquidity, diversification (standard constraints)
```

### Example: Constrained Optimization

**Unconstrained Portfolio** (Maximize return only):
- Return: 12%
- Portfolio Impact: $1B/year
- Portfolio TIR: 0.6
- Fossil fuel weight: 25%

**Constrained Portfolio** (Impact budget: $500M, TIR max: 0.4):
- Return: 10% (2% lower)
- Portfolio Impact: $450M/year (within budget)
- Portfolio TIR: 0.38 (within limit)
- Fossil fuel weight: 8%

**Trade-off**: Accept 2% lower return to halve impact and reduce financial risk (TIR)

## 3. Adaptive Management

### Trigger-Based Adjustments

**Trigger 1: TIR Exceeds Threshold**
- **Condition**: Portfolio TIR > 0.5 for 2 consecutive quarters
- **Action**: Immediate review by CRO + CFO, portfolio rebalancing plan, Board escalation

**Trigger 2: Target Off-Track**
- **Condition**: Annual impact reduction <50% of linear trajectory to 2030 target
- **Action**: Root cause analysis, revised engagement strategy, potential divestments

**Trigger 3: Scenario Deviation**
- **Condition**: Actual policy/market developments diverge significantly from SSP2-4.5 baseline
- **Action**: Scenario Committee review, potential scenario re-selection, plan adjustment

## 1. The Architecture of the Nature-Positive Transition

### 1.1 The Three Pillars

The transition to a nature-positive economy requires harmonization of three global frameworks:

1. **Global Biodiversity Framework (GBF)**: Sets sovereign regulatory ambition through 23 targets
2. **Taskforce on Nature-related Financial Disclosures (TNFD)**: Translates GBF ambition into corporate reporting standards
3. **ENCORE (Exploring Natural Capital Opportunities, Risks and Exposure)**: Provides biophysical evidence base for dependencies

**Strategic Integration**: These three frameworks create a "data supply chain" for nature:
```
Satellite/Field Data → ENCORE (Dependencies) → TNFD (Corporate Disclosure) → GBF (National Aggregation)
```

### 1.2 Why This Matters for Playbook 7

**Without harmonization**, companies face:
- Conflicting reporting requirements
- Difficulty setting science-based targets
- Inability to aggregate corporate actions to national goals

**With harmonization** (this section), companies can:
- Set SBTN targets aligned to GBF indicators
- Disclose via TNFD with confidence of GBF compliance
- Use ENCORE to identify material dependencies before they become risks

---

## 2. Global Biodiversity Framework (GBF): The Regulatory Baseline

### 2.1 GBF Monitoring Framework Architecture

Adopted December 2022 (Kunming-Montreal), the GBF establishes:
- **23 Targets** for 2030
- **4 Goals** for 2050
- **Monitoring Framework** with headline, component, and complementary indicators

**Hierarchical Structure**:
1. **Headline Indicators**: Minimum set for national reporting (global aggregation)
2. **Component Indicators**: Optional detailed metrics
3. **Complementary Indicators**: Thematic deep-dives

### 2.2 Key GBF Indicators Directly Linked to Corporate Action

#### Goal A: Ecosystem Integrity

**Headline Indicator A.2: Extent of Natural Ecosystems**
- **Measures**: Spatial area of natural ecosystems (terrestrial, freshwater, marine) remaining vs. baseline
- **Corporate Link**: Corporate land use change (Playbook 2, Section 4) aggregates to national performance on A.2
- **Strategic Implication**: "No Conversion" and "Zero Deforestation" commitments directly contribute to A.2

**Headline Indicator A.1: Red List of Ecosystems**
- **Measures**: Risk of ecosystem collapse (Least Concern → Collapsed)
- **Corporate Link**: Ecosystem condition (Playbook 2, TNFD Metric C1.1)
- **Strategic Implication**: Prioritize conservation/restoration in high-risk ecosystems

#### Goal B: Nature's Contributions to People (Ecosystem Services)

**Headline Indicator B.1: Services Provided by Ecosystems**
- **Measures**: Trends in provisioning (water, biomass), regulating (climate, flood), and cultural services
- **Corporate Link**: Direct mirror of "Dependency" analysis in TNFD and ENCORE
- **Strategic Implication**: If B.1 declines for a service you depend on (e.g., water purification), systemic risk increases

#### Target 7: Pollution

**Headline Indicator 7.1: Index of Coastal Eutrophication Potential**
- **Measures**: Nutrient loading (N, P) causing eutrophication in coastal waters
- **Corporate Link**: TNFD Metric C2.1 (Wastewater discharged) provides N/P concentrations
- **Strategic Implication**: Agriculture and wastewater sectors must reduce nutrient discharges

**Headline Indicator 7.2: Pesticide Environment Concentration**
- **Measures**: Environmental risk from pesticides (toxicity-based, not just volume)
- **Corporate Link**: TNFD Metric C2.0 (Pollutants released to soil)
- **Strategic Implication**: Agriculture must track and reduce pesticide toxicity loads

#### Target 15: Corporate Disclosure Mandate

**Requirement**: Parties must take measures to encourage business to:
1. Assess and report risks, dependencies, and impacts on biodiversity
2. Provide information to consumers for sustainable consumption

**Corporate Link**: TNFD is explicitly recognized as the primary mechanism to operationalize Target 15

**Strategic Implication**: TNFD disclosure is not just best practice - it's a GBF compliance requirement

## 3. TNFD Framework: Operationalizing GBF for Corporations

### 3.1 The LEAP Approach (Integrated Assessment)

**LEAP** = Locate, Evaluate, Assess, Prepare

1. **Locate**: Identify interface with nature
   - **Tool**: Spatial data compatible with GBF Indicator 1.1 (Spatial Plans)
   - **Outcome**: Pinpoint where assets/value chains touch high-integrity ecosystems

2. **Evaluate**: Identify dependencies and impacts
   - **Tool**: ENCORE to map economic activities to ecosystem services
   - **Outcome**: Materiality screening (which dependencies are business-critical?)

3. **Assess**: Analyze risks (physical, transition, systemic) and opportunities
   - **Tool**: Scenario analysis (Playbook 3)
   - **Outcome**: Financial risk quantification (TIR from Playbook 6)

4. **Prepare**: Report and set targets
   - **Tool**: SBTN methodologies
   - **Outcome**: TNFD disclosure + science-based targets

### 3.2 TNFD Core Global Metrics → GBF Alignment

**Deliberate One-to-One Mapping** ensures corporate disclosures aggregate to national progress:

| TNFD Core Global Metric | Definition | Corresponding GBF Indicator | Alignment Mechanism |
|------------------------|------------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| **C1.0: Extent of land/freshwater/ocean use change** | Area (ha) of ecosystem converted by type | **A.2: Extent of natural ecosystems** | Corporate-level conversion aggregates to national A.2 status |
| **C1.1: Ecosystem condition** | Degradation/improvement of ecosystem integrity relative to baseline | **A.1: Red List of Ecosystems**<br>**B.1: Services provided** | Corporate condition data feeds national assessments of ecosystem risk (A.1) and service capability (B.1) |
| **C2.0: Pollutants released to soil** | Volume (tonnes) of non-GHG pollutants (pesticides, heavy metals) | **Target 7.2: Pesticide environment concentration** | Corporate chemical loading allows states to calculate environmental concentrations and toxicity risks |
| **C2.1: Wastewater discharged** | Volume (m³) and concentration of pollutants (N, P) | **Target 7.1: Index of coastal eutrophication** | Nutrient loading from corporate wastewater is primary driver of eutrophication index |
| **C3.0: Water withdrawal from water-scarce areas** | Volume (m³) from high baseline water stress areas | **Goal B / Component: Level of water stress** | Corporate consumption in stressed basins links to "Sustainable Use" mandate of Goal B |
| **C4.0: Plastic footprint** | Total weight (tonnes) of plastic used or sold | **Target 7: Plastic pollution** | Directly supports GBF target to eliminate plastic pollution |
| **C6.0: Revenue from nature-positive products/services** | Revenue ($) from products that regenerate nature | **Target 19: Resource Mobilization** | Tracks private capital flow toward nature-positive outcomes (GBF financial targets) |

**Strategic Implication**: When a company reports TNFD Metric C1.0 (land conversion), it's not just corporate disclosure - it's contributing data to national GBF Target A.2 compliance.

### 3.3 State of Nature Metrics

**Beyond Impact Drivers** (what companies do), TNFD requires "State of Nature" metrics (what nature is):
- **Species extinction risk**
- **Ecosystem integrity index**

**Purpose**: Contextualize impact. A small impact in a critical ecosystem (high A.1 score) is more material than a large impact in a degraded one.

**Strategic Alignment**: Links corporate risk management to GBF's focus on "Key Biodiversity Areas" (KBAs)

## 4. ENCORE Database: Establishing the Scientific Dependency

### 4.1 The Taxonomy of Economic-Nature Interface

ENCORE maps:
1. **Economic Activities**: 114 sub-sectors (based on ISIC classification)
2. **Ecosystem Services**: 21 services categorized into:
   - **Provisioning** (water supply, biomass, genetic materials)
   - **Regulating** (climate, flood mitigation, erosion control, soil quality)
   - **Cultural** (recreation, aesthetic)
3. **Natural Capital Assets**: Physical stocks (Atmosphere, Water, Soil, Species)
4. **Impact Drivers**: Pressures (Water use, Land use, GHG emissions, Pollution)

### 4.2 Dependency Establishment (The Validation)

**Question**: Does ENCORE establish dependencies?

**Answer**: **Yes**. ENCORE explicitly rates dependencies from "Very Low" to "Very High" for every major economic sector.

#### Case Study 1: Water Supply Dependency

**The Dependency**: ENCORE identifies "Water Supply" as a distinct ecosystem service

**Materiality Rating**:
- **Agriculture**: Very High dependency
- **Mining**: Very High dependency
- **Beverage Manufacturing**: Very High dependency
- **Semiconductor Manufacturing**: Very High dependency

**The Link to Risk**:
- Company depends on water supply (ENCORE)
- + Water stress threatens supply (GBF Component Indicator: water stress)
- = Company faces material **transition risk** (must adapt or face operational shutdown)

**TNFD Metric Triggered**: C3.0 (Water withdrawal from water-scarce areas)

#### Case Study 2: Climate Regulation Dependency

**The Dependency**: ENCORE lists "Global Climate Regulation" and "Flood Mitigation" as ecosystem services

**Materiality Rating**:
- **Real Estate**: High dependency (flood mitigation)
- **Infrastructure**: High dependency (flood mitigation)
- **Transportation**: High dependency (stable climate)

**The Link to Risk**:
- Sectors depend on flood mitigation provided by wetlands/mangroves (ENCORE)
- + Degradation of wetlands (GBF A.2 extent reduction)
- = Reduced flood mitigation service (GBF B.1 reduction)
- = **Physical risk** for dependent sectors (flood damage increases)

**Strategic Response**: Invest in Natural Flood Management (NFM) - Example: UK Wyre Catchment (Playbook 3, Section 6.2)

#### Case Study 3: Soil Quality Dependency

**The Dependency**: ENCORE identifies "Soil quality" and "Erosion control" as ecosystem services

**Materiality Rating**:
- **Forestry**: High dependency
- **Agriculture**: Very High dependency

**The Link to Risk**:
- Agriculture depends on soil quality (ENCORE)
- + Pollution/degradation of soil (TNFD Metric C2.0)
- = Company degrades the asset it depends on
- = **Systemic risk** (productivity decline)

**Strategic Response**: Regenerative agriculture (Playbook 3, Kering Case Study, Section 3.2)

### 4.3 Impact Drivers vs. Dependencies (Double Materiality)

**Critical Distinction**:
- **Impact Drivers**: What the company does to nature (e.g., Water use, Land use, GHG emissions)
  - Maps to TNFD Metrics C1.0-C3.0
  - Determines **Impact Materiality** (ESRS, Playbook 7, Part B)

- **Dependencies**: What nature does for the company (e.g., Water supply, Flood mitigation)
  - Maps to TNFD Strategy Pillar (risk assessment)
  - Determines **Financial Materiality** (ESRS, Playbook 7, Part B)

**Double Materiality Outcome**:
- High Impact (C2.0: soil pollution) + High Dependency (ENCORE: soil quality) = **MANDATORY full disclosure + financial quantification + transition plan**

- SSP5-8.5 (crisis): -$5,000M (catastrophic post-2050)

**Scenario-Weighted NPV**:
```
NPV_weighted = 0.30 × (-$3,200M) + 0.50 × (-$1,500M) + 0.15 × (+$800M) + 0.05 × (-$5,000M)
             = -$960M - $750M + $120M - $250M
             = -$1,840M
```

**Decision**: **DO NOT INVEST** (deeply negative NPV across all realistic scenarios)

**Alternative**: Invest $500M in renewable portfolio instead:
- SSP1: +$2,000M (high demand, green subsidies)
- SSP2: +$1,200M (moderate growth)
- SSP5 (early): +$600M (limited subsidy, but still viable)
- SSP5 (crisis): +$800M (emergency renewable buildout post-crisis)

**Scenario-Weighted NPV (Renewables)**:
```
NPV_weighted = 0.30 × $2,000M + 0.50 × $1,200M + 0.20 × $700M
             = $600M + $600M + $140M
             = +$1,340M
```

**Decision**: **INVEST IN RENEWABLES** (positive NPV across all scenarios, captures upside in SSP1, limits downside in SSP5)

## 4. SSP-Specific Risk Mitigation

### 4.1 Hedging SSP5 Tail Risk

**Problem**: Even with SSP1-aligned strategy, residual exposures may remain (transition assets held short-term, physical assets that cannot be immediately sold)

**Hedging Instruments**:

**1. Physical Risk Insurance** (Coastal Real Estate)
```
Asset: $500M coastal property (cannot divest immediately due to illiquidity)
SSP5 Risk: $2.8B contingent liability (uninsurable post-2050)

Hedge: Parametric flood insurance (2025-2035 period)
- Premium: $5M/year × 10 years = $50M
- Payout: $200M (if 100-year flood becomes 10-year flood)
- Coverage: Partial (40% of SSP2 risk, SSP5 uninsurable)

Net Risk Reduction: $200M coverage - $50M premium = $150M protected
Residual Risk: $2.6B (SSP5 crisis still catastrophic, but delayed exit possible)
```

**2. Carbon Price Put Options** (Transition Assets)
```
Asset: Oil & gas holdings ($1,000M, gradual divestment 2025-2035)
SSP5 Risk: $3B contingent liability (crisis carbon tax)

Hedge: Put options on carbon-intensive stock index
- Cost: $10M/year × 10 years = $100M
- Payout: $500M (if carbon tax spike triggers 50% index decline)

Net Risk Reduction: $400M protected
```

**3. Biodiversity Credits** (Land Use Exposure)
```
Asset: Agriculture with legacy deforestation (2010-2020, pre-EUDR)
SSP5 Risk: $1.95B retroactive liability (Amazon tipping point)

Hedge: Purchase biodiversity credits for historical footprint
- Cost: 10,000 ha × $10,000/ha (credit price) = $100M
- Offset: Legal defense (demonstrate proactive restoration commitment)

Net Risk Reduction: Legal/reputational protection, may reduce retroactive penalties by 30-50%
```

**Total Hedging Budget**: $50M + $100M + $100M = **$250M** (over 10 years)

**Hedging Effectiveness**:
- **SSP2 Scenario**: Hedges largely unnecessary (moderate risk), $250M cost is insurance premium
- **SSP5 Early**: Hedges underperform (physical risk hasn't materialized), $250M cost exceeds benefit
- **SSP5 Crisis**: Hedges provide **$1.05B payout** (vs. $7B residual exposures) → **15% risk reduction**

**Conclusion**: Hedging provides **limited protection** against SSP5 tail (most SSP5 risks are unhedgeable) → **Reinforces primacy of divestment over hedging**.
